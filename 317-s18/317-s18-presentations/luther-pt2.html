Spiritualists

  • did not try to impose themselves on a territory
  • withdraw in closed communities (cf. Carlstadt & Thomas in Müntzer)

Anti-Trinitarians

  • started 1550's in Poland & Hungary
  • like Arius? hold Christ divine but not equal to Father
  • Saustus Socinus (Italy) influenced east Europeans – denied Christ's divinity
  • distinguished by tolerance
  • difficulty in 16th c. to expect diversity within a territory

Lutheran Developments

  • Luther's influence: translating the Bible & getting married
  • "priesthood of all believers"
  • systematizing of Melanchthon – caution & moderation
  • compromoise on nonessentials (adiaphora)
  • "we are justified though we remain sinners because God imputes Christ's righteousness to us" (Melanchthon)
  • infused righteousness vs. imputed righteousness
  • unqualified emphasis on Grace (Luther) – vs. later modified that we can cooperate with Grace (cf. Strigel)
  • Formula of Concord is expressed Lutheran synthesis – passion missing. systematizing Luther's thought somehow changed them.

Jeremiah

  • His life itself is a sign (cp. Hosea) – the one symbolized Israel’s infidelity, the other Israel’s “desolation”
  • J’s lamentation are personal and passionate (Jer 15:15-21 & Jer 20:7-18)
  • Jer 31 gives us the “name” of the Christian scriptures: “I will make a new covenant …” (New Testament)

Exekiel

  • like J. he lived before, during, and after the destruction of Jerusalem – though he is younger
  • Ezekiel went early into exile (he was upper class) and so prophesied from Babylon – J. prophesied from Jerusalem
  • Israel has sinned and so God has left the city
  • “vision” of restoration – dry bones given the breath to live again

2nd Temple

  • prophecy comes to an end
  • Prophecy gives way to apocalyptic
